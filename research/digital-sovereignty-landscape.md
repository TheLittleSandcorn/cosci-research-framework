# Digital Sovereignty Landscape

## Executive Summary

Digital sovereignty is becoming one of the defining infrastructure challenges of the 21st century.

Modern societies increasingly depend on:

- hyperscale cloud providers
- centralized AI ecosystems
- proprietary infrastructure layers
- platform-controlled identity systems
- externally governed data architectures

This creates structural dependencies with long-term implications for:

- privacy
- infrastructure resilience
- operational autonomy
- democratic control
- economic independence
- knowledge sovereignty

---

# Core Definition

## Digital Sovereignty

Digital sovereignty describes the capability of individuals, organizations, and societies to:

- control their own infrastructure
- govern their own data
- maintain operational independence
- reduce external technological dependency
- preserve long-term infrastructure resilience

---

# Core Structural Drivers

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|

| Proprietary Ecosystems | closed infrastructure architectures |
| Knowledge Fragmentation | loss of maintainable infrastructure knowledge |

It enables:

- infrastructure ownership
- local control

- data autonomy

- reproducible environments
